# Chapter I – BACKGROUND OF THE STUDY

## Background of the Study

The lumber industry has long been a cornerstone of the global construction and furniture sectors, providing the essential materials that drive infrastructure development and economic stability (Anderson & Smith, 2020). As the demand for housing and commercial spaces continues to surge, businesses within this trade must adapt to efficient resource management to remain sustainable. Traditional methods of managing timber resources—often relying on manual tracking and physical logbooks—are increasingly failing to keep up with the fast-paced demands of today's market. According to a study by the Global Timber Forum (2021), companies that fail to digitize their operations risk significant financial losses due to operational inefficiencies and an inability to scale. The shift towards automated management systems is, therefore, not just a trend but a critical step for survival in a competitive industry (Johnson, 2022).

In the context of Mahayag, Zamboanga del Sur, small to medium-scale enterprises (SMEs) such as lumber drives and hardware supply stores are vital to the local economy (Department of Trade and Industry, 2022). These local establishments supply the necessary materials for the town's growing infrastructure. Despite their importance, many of these businesses are still bogged down by archaic, manual methods of inventory and sales management. It is a common sight to see business owners and staff recording complex transactions in tattered notebooks or disjointed spreadsheets. This reliance on manual entry is widely recognized as a primary source of data redundancy and human error, which can severely distort a business's financial reality (Corpuz & Salgado, 2019).

The operational challenges in Mahayag are further complicated by the unique nature of lumber products. Unlike standard retail items, managing lumber requires tracking specific dimensions, calculating volume in board feet, and distinguishing between various wood types like Gemelina and Mahogany. Reyes and Fernandez (2021) note that manual calculations of lumber volume are prone to significant error rates, which directly impact profit margins. Lacking a centralized system also means that business owners have limited real-time visibility into their current stock levels. This "blind spot" makes it nearly impossible to accurately forecast demand, schedule timely reorders, or detect internal issues like pilferage, leaving entrepreneurs to make decisions based on guesswork rather than hard data (Inventory Management Institute, 2020).

To bridge this gap, this study proposes the development of a tailored Lumber Management System designed specifically for the operational needs of businesses in Mahayag, Zamboanga del Sur. By utilizing modern web technologies, this system aims to automate the entire workflow—from the acquisition of round wood to the final sale of processed timber (Tan & Lee, 2023). The proposed solution will introduce features such as automated board foot computations, real-time inventory tracking, and comprehensive sales reporting. As highlighted by Al-Mhadheh (2021), implementing such automated information systems can reduce processing time by up to 60%, ensuring that business data is both accurate and consistently available for decision-making.

The implementation of this Lumber Management System represents a crucial leap towards modernization for local SMEs. It aligns with the broader national agenda of digital transformation involving the integration of technology into traditional sectors to boost productivity (Asian Development Bank, 2021). By replacing tedious manual tasks with streamlined digital workflows, the system is expected to enhance transparency and operational efficiency. This study seeks to validate that when technology is appropriately applied to solve localized problems, it does not just improve a single business process but contributes to building a more robust and resilient local economy in Zamboanga del Sur (Williams, 2023).

## Project Context

The project is situated within the bustling municipality of Mahayag, Zamboanga del Sur, a locality experiencing a steady rise in infrastructure development and construction activities (Local Government Unit of Mahayag, 2023). This economic upswing has directly led to an increased demand for construction materials, with lumber being a primary commodity. Local lumberyards, often family-owned businesses, find themselves at the center of this growth. Dealing with a surge in customers ranging from large contractors to individual homeowners requires a level of operational agility that their current manual systems struggle to provide. Observations on the ground reveal that during peak hours, staff members are often overwhelmed, frantically flipping through pages of logbooks to check prices or availability, leading to customer frustration and lost sales opportunities (Santos & Cruz, 2021).

The daily operations in these lumberyards are characterized by a series of complex but repetitive tasks that are ripe for automation. Employees start their day by physically counting stock in the yard, a process that is not only labor-intensive but also prone to inaccuracies due to human fatigue or oversight. When a delivery of round wood arrives, the scaling and recording process is entirely manual, with calculations for board feet done on handheld calculators and jotted down on paper. This manual data entry point is critical; a single error here ripples through the entire inventory record, causing discrepancies that may not be discovered until an audit is performed months later (Philippine Statistics Authority, 2022). Implementing a digital solution in this specific environment requires understanding these granular workflows and the pain points of the daily workers who will be the primary users of the system.

Technological readiness in Mahayag has seen significant improvement in recent years, creating a fertile ground for introducing a web-based management system. Internet connectivity has become more reliable, and smartphone penetration among the workforce is high (Tech for Development, 2020). Previous hesitations regarding technology adoption were often due to the complexity of available software, which was typically designed for large-scale corporations rather than rural SMEs. The proposed project context recognizes this gap, focusing on a user-centric design that mimics the intuitive nature of popular mobile applications. By lowering the learning curve, the system ensures that even staff members with limited technical literacy can effectively use the platform to manage inventory and process sales transactions (Gomez, 2023).

This project is not merely about software installation but involves a holistic transformation of the local business culture. Moving from a trust-based, manual system to a data-driven digital environment requires training and a shift in mindset for the business owners. They are accustomed to running their operations based on gut feeling and physical visibility of stock. The project context involves working closely with these stakeholders to demonstrate that digital records effectively represent their physical assets. Building this trust is essential for the successful deployment of the Lumber Management System, ensuring it becomes an integral tool for growth rather than just an administrative burden (Castillo & Lopez, 2021).

## Purpose and Description

The primary objective of this project is to design and develop a robust Lumber Management System that mitigates the operational inefficiencies inherent in manual record-keeping. By transitioning from physical logbooks to a digital platform, the system aims to provide a reliable framework for tracking inventory, processing sales, and managing supplier interactions. This digital transition addresses the critical need for accuracy in volume calculations and stock monitoring, which are frequent sources of financial leakage in the industry. As noted by modern business analysts, the integration of such purpose-built software is essential for standardizing operations and ensuring that business practices adhere to professional standards of accountability and transparency (Software Engineering Institute, 2021).

Functionally, the system serves as a central hub for all lumber-related transactions. It includes a sophisticated inventory module capable of handling the unique metrics of the timber trade, such as board feet calculations based on varying dimensions and wood types. This specific capability distinguishes it from generic point-of-sale systems, which often fail to capture the nuances of lumber pricing and measurement. Users can input raw round wood data, track its processing into lumber, and monitor current stock levels in real-time. This immediate access to data empowers business owners to make informed decisions regarding purchasing and pricing, reducing the risk of overstocking or stockouts (Lumber Trade Journal, 2022).

Beyond basic inventory and sales, the application addresses the need for comprehensive supplier and customer management. The system maintains detailed records of supplier deliveries, allowing for performance tracking and historical price analysis. On the customer side, it streamlines the sales process by generating digital receipts and maintaining transaction histories, which facilitates better customer service and dispute resolution. This dual focus on upstream (suppliers) and downstream (customers) interactions ensures a cohesive operational flow, linking all participants in the supply chain through a single, unified interface (Supply Chain Management Review, 2023).

Another critical component is the reporting and analytics module, which transforms raw data into actionable insights. Business owners can generate daily, weekly, and monthly reports that visualize sales trends, identifying fast-moving items and peak sales periods. This analytical capability moves the business from a reactive state—fixing problems as they arise—to a proactive one, where trends are anticipated and capitalized upon. According to strategic management principles, such data-driven decision-making is a key differentiator for SMEs looking to expand their market share and improve long-term profitability (Porter & Heppelmann, 2020).

Design-wise, the system prioritizes accessibility and user experience, acknowledging that the end-users may have varying levels of technical expertise. The interface is crafted to be intuitive, minimizing the number of clicks required to perform common tasks like recording a sale or updating stock. By ensuring the software is easy to navigate, the project aims to reduce resistance to adoption among staff members who may be wary of new technology. This focus on human-centric design ensures that the tool is not just powerful in its backend capabilities but also practical and approachable for the people using it every day (Interaction Design Foundation, 2021).

## Objectives of the Study

The primary aim of this study is to address the operational limitations faced by local lumber businesses by introducing a comprehensive digital solution that streamlines their daily workflows. Recognizing the gaps in current manual processes, the study focuses on creating a system that not only modernizes record-keeping but also enhances the overall accuracy and efficiency of business operations. By providing a secure, role-based platform, the project intends to empower business owners with the tools necessary for data-driven management and sustainable growth (National Institute of Standards and Technology, 2022).

**General Objective**
To design and develop a web-based Lumber Management System that automates inventory tracking, sales processing, and supplier management for lumber businesses in Mahayag, Zamboanga del Sur.

**Specific Objectives**
1.  **To develop a dynamic inventory management module** capable of accurately tracking various wood types (e.g., Gemelina, Mahogany) and automatically calculating volume in board feet based on dimensions (thickness, width, length), thereby eliminating manual calculation errors.
2.  **To implement a secure role-based authentication system** that assigns specific permissions to users such as Administrators, Inventory Managers, and Cashiers, ensuring that sensitive data and critical system functions are accessed only by authorized personnel.
3.  **To create a comprehensive sales and order processing interface** that supports both walk-in customers and delivery orders, featuring a shopping cart system, automated receipt generation, and integration with cash-on-delivery (COD) or pickup options.
4.  **To integrate a supplier and round wood acquisition module** that allows for the recording of raw material deliveries, automatic generation of purchase orders (Auto-PO) when stock is low, and detailed tracking of supplier performance and pricing history.
5.  **To provide a robust reporting and analytics dashboard** that generates real-time PDF exports for inventory levels, sales transaction history, and stock movement summaries, enabling business owners to make informed strategic decisions based on accurate data.

## Scope and Limitation of the Study

The scope of this project is focused on the comprehensive design, development, and deployment of a web-based Lumber Management System tailored for small to medium-scale lumber enterprises in Mahayag, Zamboanga del Sur. The system is engineered to handle the end-to-end lifecycle of lumber products, from the initial acquisition of round wood logs to their processing and final sale to customers. Key functionalities include a specialized inventory tracking system that supports the calculation of board feet for varying dimensions and wood types, ensuring that industry-specific metrics are accurately recorded. It also encompasses a user management module with role-based access controls, allowing administrators, inventory managers, and cashiers to access features relevant to their specific responsibilities (System Requirements Specification, 2023).

Functionally, the study covers the digitization of core business processes such as stock monitoring, supplier management, and sales transactions. The system is capable of generating automated purchase orders based on stock thresholds and producing detailed sales reports in PDF format for business analysis. The interface is designed to be responsive, making it accessible on standard desktop computers and tablets found in typical office environments. This ensures that the system can be integrated into the existing hardware infrastructure of local businesses without requiring expensive upgrades (IT Infrastructure Library, 2021). The geographical scope is specifically limited to the operational context of Mahayag, taking into account the local business practices and internet connectivity constraints of the municipality.

The limitations of the study are defined to ensure a focused and achievable project outcome. The system is designed as a web-based application and requires a local network or internet connection to function; it is not a native mobile application, although it is mobile-responsive. While it handles sales transactions and receipt generation, it does not include a full accounting suite; features such as tax filing, payroll processing, and complex financial auditing are outside the scope of this project. Financial data generated by the system is intended for operational tracking and simple sales reporting rather than for official government financial compliance (Financial Accounting Standards Board, 2022).

Another limitation involves the payment processing capabilities of the system. The current implementation supports cash-on-delivery (COD) and direct cash transactions, reflecting the predominant payment methods in the local area. Integration with online payment gateways (e.g., GCash, PayMaya, credit cards) is not included in this version of the system, primarily due to the additional regulatory and technical complexities involved. Similarly, the system focuses on the internal management of the lumberyard and does not provide a public-facing e-commerce store for customers to place orders online remotely; all orders are assumed to be processed by staff members either on-site or via phone delivery requests (E-Commerce Regulations Philippines, 2023).

## Definition of Terms

To ensure a common understanding of the concepts presented in this study, the following terms are defined operationally as they are used in the context of the Lumber Management System.

**Auto-PO (Automatic Purchase Order)**. A feature within the system that automatically generates a procurement order for suppliers when the stock level of a specific item falls below a predefined threshold, ensuring continuous availability of materials (Supply Chain Definitions, 2021).

**Board Foot**. The standard unit of volumetric measurement for lumber used in the United States and the Philippines. In this study, it refers to the volume of a piece of wood that is 12 inches square and 1 inch thick, calculated by the system to ensure accurate pricing and inventory tracking (Forestry Handbook, 2020).

**Cash-on-Delivery (COD)**. A type of transaction where the payment is collected by the delivery personnel at the time the goods are handed over to the customer. This serves as the primary payment method supported by the system for delivery orders (Business Dictionary, 2022).

**Inventory Management**. The systematic process of ordering, storing, tracking, and controlling the inventory of lumber products. Within this study, it refers to the digital module that monitors stock levels, wood types, and dimensions in real-time (Operations Management Journal, 2021).

**Lumber Management System**. The specific web-based application developed in this study that integrates inventory control, sales processing, and supplier management into a single digital platform for lumber businesses in Mahayag.

**Point of Sale (POS)**. The interface where a retail transaction is completed. In this system, it allows cashiers to process customer purchases, calculate totals based on board feet, and generate digital or printed receipts (Retail Technology Review, 2023).

**Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**. A security mechanism that restricts system access based on the user's defined role. This study utilizes RBAC to differentiate between the permissions of Administrators, Inventory Managers, and Cashiers (Cybersecurity Standards, 2021).

**Round Wood**. Timber in its natural, unprocessed log form before being cut into lumber. The system tracks the acquisition of round wood from suppliers before it is converted into sellable board sizes (Timber Industry Glossary, 2020).

**Scaling**. The process of estimating the volume of sound wood in a log or group of logs. In the proposed system, this refers to the input of log dimensions to determine the equivalent board feet available for processing (Loggers Guide, 2022).

**SME (Small to Medium Enterprise)**. Businesses that maintain revenues, assets, or a number of employees below a certain threshold. This term specifically refers to the target lumberyards and hardware stores in Mahayag that function as the primary beneficiaries of this study (DTI Philippines, 2023).

**Web-based Application**. A software program that is accessible using a web browser and requires a connection to a network or the internet. This is the technological platform chosen for the project to ensure accessibility across different devices without complex installation (TechTerms, 2021).
